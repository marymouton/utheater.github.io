Night of January 16th

in collaboration with Windmill Chinese Drama Club
written by Ayn Rand, translated by Qi Zheng
directed by Qi Zheng

On January 16th, 1934, near midnight, the body of a man came hurtling down and crashed at the foot of the Faulkner Building in Manhattan. What happened on that fateful night that led to a business tycoon's tragic death? In this murder mystery, you will attend a court hearing in 1930's, ponder contradicting testimonies, and join us in a jury that delivers the verdict of a century. 80 years after the play's Broadway debut, Windmill Drama revitalizes this Ayn Rand play's original splendor in Mandarin Chinese, offering the audience a theatre experience like no other. The performance is in Mandarin Chinese with detailed English synopsis provided. Everyone is welcome. Â
